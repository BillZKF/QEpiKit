Hierarchal Task Network Examples

HTNs are a planning tool for evaluating the suitablity of a multiple of methods for accomplishing a task. In the process, a main task is decomposed into sub-tasks. There are two main types of nodes: Methods and Operators. Operators are leaf nodes, meaning they have no subtasks. Operators evaluate a precondition, then perform an action. Method nodes evaluate a precondition, and then visit subtasks. These subtasks can either be Methods or Operators.
